You found my ring! I got this from my Aunt when I graduated middle school. She knew that peace signs were my favorite symbol. I've been wearing it ever since I got it in 2008. I have never misplaced it and you can usually find it on my right ring finger. "Imagine" by John Lennon, although well known, describes a dream I too have always had for the world.

This is a tambourine that you can attach to your foot. I saw another guitarist playing with it and so I got one at Watermelon Music store in Davis. It brings back nostalgic memories of playing guitar in the freshman dorms with my two best friends. They brought a guitar and a ukelele and we would play songs together almost every weekend. "Lost in My Mind" by The Head and the Heart was not an unusual song for us to play.

This is one of my favorite sleep shirts. An album by Pink Floyd, "Dark side of the Moon," includes a clear prism and display of light splitting into rainbow colors-- a classic symbol for the band. Mostly this shirt makes me think of when I was driving with my Dad and him explaining the meaning of their song, "Mother." It's about an overprotective and skeptical mother sheltering her child, and as a result, the child finds mother building a wall, "the wall," to protect him, from girlfriends, from the government, from the world.
