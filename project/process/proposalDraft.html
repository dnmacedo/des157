ISEA Proposal Draft: Artist Talk/Work in Progress

My project for the ISEA Conference involves an interface of games that help multilinguals pick up the many quirks of English. I got the idea from an article that I read for a psychology class that I am currently taking. My class is language and cognition, and the paper I read had to do with comprehension and speech production in bilinguals vs monolinguals. This paper motivated me to explore the difficulties bilinguals face with their second language: specifically English. In the paper I learned that depending on how bilinguals were raised in early life, (being exposed to English speech or not before school teaching) it could tell a lot about the proficiency of people who are bilingual later in life. So I wanted to try and teach those quirks in English, even to people who didnâ€™t get the chance to be exposed to it when they were little, and enrich their English speaking/writing/reading experience.

The idea of the interface I will make will be comprised of different categories of games that focus on different areas in English where frustrating quirks come up. Within those categories there will be one or more games for people to interact with to help themselves pick up and notice these quirks better. For example, I want to do a simple phoneme game, where it teaches people who are bilingual how to differentiate the sounds in hearing and in speech production. Phonemes, or small pieces of whole speech sounds, can be anatomically similar to one another in speech production, like /g/ and /k/. The goal of this game is to help multilinguals differentiate between the sounds when hearing it, and also try practicing it on their own.

I have many more games in mind to explore different topics and features of the complex English language.
